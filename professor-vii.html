The Professor VII

"Prof, here's the—"

"Now put it back," said The Professor.

"What? But you just—"

She burst into a cackle. "Did you really think we'd be cutting Mr. Beckett's bones?" The Chef frowned and turned around.

"So what was that shit for?" asked The Economist.

"A little scaaare," she chirped. "But the next time's for real, okay? So be a good boy, or God knows what'll happen to you."

He stared at her. "Let's go, Soldier!" said The Professor.

"Were you serious?" he whispered.

"'Course not," she answered. "I'm going to do something else."

"What is that? I'll help."

"I'm not sure just yet. I've been scratching my head since lunch..." she said. "Sorry, speaking of the time, can I trust you to see the footage on your own? I want to check on somebody else."

"Of course, ma'am."

"Psh, you're making me feel old," she said. "Feel free to call me if you need help!"

She went back and didn't pay The Economist even a look; she entered the quarters. Someone was asleep in the quarters at least two or three hours ago; he'd better be awake by now. But no, The Engineer was snoring and laid by his side on the top bunk.

"ENGINEER!"

He stayed silent, so she shook him by the shoulder. "Yoohoo? Engineeeeeer!"

No matter what she did, he wouldn't wake up. She sighed, jumped down, and walked away.

She entered the dining hall. "You're early, April."

"I should be the one saying that," she answered. "Can I ask you for a verdict, Maria?"

"A verdict?" said The Doctor. "On what?"

"The Economist got into a fight with The Chef less than an hour ago," said The Professor. "And I mean a physical fight. What do you say we—"

"Is she okay?"

"She's stronger than we both think, I'll admit that," said The Professor. "There's a little bruising on her cheek, I'm afraid."

"Where's she now?"

"In the morgue."

The Doctor swallowed. "I'm sorry?"

"Kidding, I actually don't know. All I know is she isn't in the quarters. She's fine, though, she could still carry a bonesaw!"

"A bonesaw? Why was she holding that?"

"Do you expect me to know?" she replied. 

They both paused. "The morgue wasn't a joke, was it?"

"Ding ding ding!" she whistled. "And?"

"And you're hiding something."

"Well, if I am, I'm not gonna 'fess it up on my own, am I?"

She gave her a mad gaze. "You're not going to tell me she held it in self-defence, are you, April?"

"Oh, that's exactly what happened!"

"April," she sternly repeated. "If you don't tell me the truth, humanity won't progress. And we won't exit this spaceship alive."

"On the contrary, I don't really mind dying right now, but as an educator, if Nathan and Jill and Isabelle or their children all managed to live until the end, then I think that's still a win, isn't it?"

"You're playing dumb again. That, or I need to remind you that I'm—" 

"The Captain's gone now. You can show Jill everything you know, can't you?"

The Doctor sighed. "Forget it. I'm cooking dinner myself."
